<p><ul><li>Continuous Fibers</li><li>Discontinuous (Short) Fibers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Glass Fibre Reinforced Plastic (GFRP) market is categorized into two main types: continuous fibers and discontinuous (short) fibers. Continuous fibers involve long strands of glass fibers, providing superior mechanical strength and durability, ideal for structural applications. In contrast, discontinuous fibers consist of shorter glass fibers, which enhance flexibility and are often used in products requiring less rigidity, such as molded parts. Each type serves distinct applications, catering to various industries including automotive, construction, and consumer goods.</p></p>

<p><p>The rechargeable lithium-air battery market comprises three main types: non-aqueous, aqueous, and solid. Non-aqueous lithium-air batteries utilize organic solvents, enabling higher energy density, but face challenges like stability and efficiency. Aqueous lithium-air batteries use water-based electrolytes, offering safety and environmental benefits, yet they struggle with lower performance and corrosion issues. Solid lithium-air batteries incorporate solid electrolytes, presenting advantages in safety and stability, but are still in development, aiming to combine the benefits of both non-aqueous and aqueous systems.</p></p>

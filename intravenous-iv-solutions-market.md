<p><p>Intravenous (IV) solutions are essential for delivering nutrients and medications directly into the bloodstream. In the market, two primary types are available: Peripheral Parenteral Nutrition (PPN) and Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN). PPN is used for patients requiring short-term nutritional support via peripheral veins, offering a balanced mix of macronutrients and micronutrients. Conversely, TPN is designed for long-term nutritional needs, delivered through central veins, providing comprehensive nutrition when the gastrointestinal tract is non-functioning or inaccessible.</p></p>

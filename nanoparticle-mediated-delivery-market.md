<p><p>Nanoparticle-mediated delivery systems are classified into two main market types based on their origin: biological and non-biological. Biological origin nanoparticles, such as liposomes and exosomes, are derived from natural sources and are often used for targeted drug delivery due to their biocompatibility and ability to mimic cellular environments. In contrast, non-biological origin nanoparticles, like polymeric and inorganic nanoparticles, are synthetically produced and can be engineered for specific therapeutic applications. Both types offer unique advantages, enhancing drug efficacy and reducing side effects.</p></p>

<p><p>The cDNA library market is primarily categorized into phage and plasmid libraries. Phage libraries utilize bacteriophages to display cloned cDNA fragments, allowing efficient screening for specific proteins or genes. In contrast, plasmid libraries employ plasmids as vectors to replicate and store cDNA, facilitating easier manipulation and analysis. Both types are essential for genomic research, gene expression studies, and drug discovery, providing researchers with diverse tools to explore genetic material and develop novel biotechnological applications.</p></p>

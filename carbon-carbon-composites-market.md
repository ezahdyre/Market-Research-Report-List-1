<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carbon-carbon composites are advanced materials made from carbon fibers reinforced in a carbon matrix, offering high strength, thermal stability, and lightweight properties. The **Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD)** process involves depositing carbon onto fibers in a gaseous environment, creating a dense, high-performance matrix. The **Liquid Impregnation Process** uses liquid precursors, such as resins or pitches, which are impregnated into fibers and then carbonized to form the matrix. Both methods are key in producing composites for aerospace, automotive, and industrial applications.</p></p>

<p><p>The glutinous rice flour market consists of two main types: dried glutinous rice flour and water milled glutinous rice flour. Dried glutinous rice flour is produced by drying and grinding glutinous rice into a fine powder, making it suitable for various culinary applications, such as dumplings and desserts. Water milled glutinous rice flour, on the other hand, is made by soaking the rice in water before milling, resulting in a smoother texture favored for traditional recipes. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences in Asian cuisines and beyond.</p></p>
